Education

Currently I am pursuing a M.S. in Computer Science at Northeastern Illinois University in Chicago. As of Fall 2018, my planned graduation date is April, 2019. The coursework I have already completed can be viewed in the "Course Work page of this website. All courses were completed With a grade of A, with the exception of Discrete Modeling and Analysis, in which I received a grade of B, bringing my cumulative graduate GPA to 3.83.

Previous to my graduate program at NEIU, I received a B.S. in Exercise Science from The University of Kansas in Lawrence, KS. I graduated from this program in December of 2013.

I graduated from New Trier High School in Winnetka, IL prior to my college career.

Career Life

My first job post-college was a job as a Rehabilitation Aide, working for Athletico Physical Therapy. During this time I had the great pleasure of working with many different types of patients, and getting to know them on a personal level. It was here that I got the invaluable experience learning to work as part of a team, where one "cog in the engine" was just as important as all the others to keep the clinic running smoothly. Multi-tasking was the norm, and making sure no patient felt neglected, and was properly cared for, was at times challenging with our high patient volume. Still I would not trade this experience for anything, as it helped me develop into a more well rounded professional, able to handle potentially stressful tasks and situations with a cool head, all the while learning a great deal about the health sciences.

While at Athletico, I transitioned from Rehabilitation Aide to the management position of Office Coordinator. This role was designed to be in charge of managing all the office's non-clinical administrative work. This included everything from managing patient charts to validating and certifying insurances. When the clinic came under new ownership, I transfered with part of my team to a new Company, while maintaining the same role. IvyRehab Physical Therapy was a PT chain popular in the East Coast, and was trying to get a foothold in the MidWest. We were opening the clinic from scratch, and so had a great deal to learn in order to get things running smoothly. The time I spent at IvyRehab taught me much more about managing a business than I had learned previously. At the same time, my muti-tasking skills were often put to the test to help the therapists with treatments, while also managing the office. It was a lot of fun learning so many new things, and I'm extremely grateful to have been given the opportunity to take part in the opening of a new business.

It was during my time working at IvyRehab that I discovered a passion for computer programming. I was allured to the aspect of being able to create something logical and functional out of my own creativity. What I think I find so enthralling about software engineering, is not only the vast amount of applications it encompasses, but also that each application and problem has more than one way to be implimented. That is, there are many means to an end in programming, and half the fun is figuring out what way works best.

As I became more serious about wanting to pursue a career in programming, I applied to NEIU to begin a masters program in Computer Science. Shortly before entering the program, I left my job in the health field so I could focus on this new skill that I wanted to master. I am very excited to begin putting my new skill to work in a career in software development.
